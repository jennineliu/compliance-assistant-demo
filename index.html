Critical Advisory

Electronic Cigarettes & Vapes Restricted

ANVISA prohibits the importation, sale, advertising, and distribution of electronic smoking devices in Brazil. Travelers should not bring vapes, pods, refills, or e-liquids, because border authorities may retain products subject to ANVISA control even though ANVISA FAQ language distinguishes personal possession from commercial activity.

Drone / Unmanned Aircraft Compliance

Mandatory protocols for entering Brazilian airspace

ANAC Registration (SISANT)

  • • Model aircraft heavier than 250g must be registered or inscribed with ANAC.
  • • Recreational and non-recreational flights follow different ANAC requirements.
  • • RETA third-party liability insurance is required for non-recreational aircraft above 250g.

ANATEL & DECEA

  • • Radio transmitters must comply with ANATEL frequency guidelines.
  • • Check DECEA airspace access rules in SARPAS before flying; authorization depends on the area and operation.
  • • Flights above 120 meters (400 ft) generally require additional licensing or authorization.

e-DBV Declaration Gateway

Travelers must submit the Electronic Traveler's Goods Declaration (e-DBV) if carrying:

Cash above USD 10,000 (or equivalent foreign currency).

Taxable purchase goods exceeding the quota limit ($1,000 / $500).

Items under sanitary, agricultural, Army, or other-agency control.
